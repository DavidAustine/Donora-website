## **Slide 4: What is Agile?**

* Based on **empirical approach**
* Accepts **complexity of the problem**
* Uses:

  * **frequent inspection**
  * **constant adaptation**

---

## **Slide 5: Agile software development**

* **Iterative and incremental development**
* Requirements evolve through:

  * **collaboration**
  * **self-organizing, cross-functional teams**
* Promotes:

  * **adaptive planning**
  * **evolutionary development**
  * **time-boxed iterative approach**
  * **rapid and flexible response to change**

## **Slide 6: Agile Manifesto (Background)**

* **2001**, 17 developers at **Snowbird, Utah**
* Created:

  * **Manifesto for Agile Software Development**
* Formed:

  * **Agile Alliance**

---

## **Slide 7: Agile Manifesto (Core Values)**

* **Individuals and interactions over processes and tools**

* **Working software over comprehensive documentation**

* **Customer collaboration over contract negotiation**

* **Responding to change over following a plan**

* Key idea:

  * Value left **more than right**

---

## **Slide 8: Meaning of Agile Values**

* **Working software**

  * **continuous involvement**

  * **analysis, design, construction, testing**
